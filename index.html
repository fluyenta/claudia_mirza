About Claudia

From Cartago
to the World Stage

Claudia Mirza was born in Cartago, Colombia to agricultural workers. Her father left for the United States when she was four to work with thoroughbred horse trainers. She and her mother moved to Medellín — into the projects, in cartel-controlled territory during the drug wars. Despite everything, her mother enrolled her in a prestigious Catholic school. That stubborn belief in education became Claudia's foundation. She arrived in America at 20, barely speaking English.

The 2002 dot-com crash cost her a corporate job at Genuity — and gave her a company. A mentor at the Greater Dallas Hispanic Chamber told her to stop job-hunting and start building. Akorbi became a $55 million global enterprise with 700 employees across twelve countries, growing 1,267% in four years. In 2021 she acquired RunMyProcess from Fujitsu. In 2024 she exited Akorbi. Today she runs RunMyProcess and Mirza Holdings — an inventor with multiple patents pending who still believes the best businesses are built on human connection.
